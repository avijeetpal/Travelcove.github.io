Mustafa Castle

History

Mustafa Castle is one of the historical and grand landmarks in the city of Meerut. Mustafa Castle was built in the memory of Nawab Mustafa Khan Shaifta which was constructed by his son Nawab Mohammad Ishaq Khan in the year 1900. This place is known for conducting political action during the freedom struggle of India. The palace has a distinctive style of architecture, displaying some of the finest furniture, paintings and artefacts of the time. If you are planning to visit Meerut then definitely visit this place. Built on 30 acres of land, this building was designed by Nawab M. Ishaq Khan. The Nawab incorporated many styles of architecture in the construction of Mustafa Castle. Mecca clay was used in the construction process and in the replacement of an internal gallows with an Islamic mosque.

Interesting Facts for tourists to visit

  • Different styles of architecture have been mixed in the construction of the palace and art and artifacts have been imported from London to display them with pride.
  • In the palace you can find some of the rare artifacts of earlier times which are well preserved such as pendulum clocks, ornately designed lamps, antique chandeliers, carved wooden cabinets and various historical paintings.

Time to Visit

Best Time to Visit: Mustafa Castle opens from 07:00 am to 06:00 pm, every day of the week.
